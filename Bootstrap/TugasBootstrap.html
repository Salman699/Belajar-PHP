Nebula

A nebula is a large cloud of dust, hydrogen gas, helium gas, and other ionized gases found in space. These clouds are often the birthplaces of stars, and they can be seen as bright or dark patches in the night sky. There are several different types of nebulae, including emission nebulae, reflection nebulae, and planetary nebulae.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and is the largest planet in our solar system. It is a gas giant, meaning it is predominantly composed of hydrogen and helium gas, with no solid surface. Jupiter is known for its distinctive colored bands of clouds that encircle the planet, as well as the iconic Great Red Spot, which is a massive storm that has been raging on Jupiter's surface for centuries.

Astronauts

Astronauts are individuals who are specially trained to operate spacecraft and carry out missions in outer space. They are typically members of a space agency, such as NASA or ESA, and undergo extensive training to prepare for space missions.
